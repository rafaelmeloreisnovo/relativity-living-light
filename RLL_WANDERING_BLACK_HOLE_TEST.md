# RLL Wandering Black Hole Lensing Test

## Hypothesis
A moving compact object should produce a time-dependent lensing signature.
RLL is testable only if it predicts a measurable residual beyond the standard GR lensing model.

## Standard GR baseline
\[
\alpha_{\mathrm{GR}} \approx \frac{4GM}{c^2 b}
\]

For a moving lens, fit at minimum:
- lens mass \(M\)
- transverse velocity \(v_t\)
- lens distance \(D_l\)
- source distance \(D_s\)
- time-varying impact parameter \(b(t)\)
- local shear/environment

## RLL observable
\[
\Delta\alpha_{\mathrm{RLL}}(t)=\alpha_{\mathrm{obs}}(t)-\alpha_{\mathrm{GR,moving\ lens}}(t)
\]

## Required prediction
RLL must explicitly specify whether \(\Delta\alpha_{\mathrm{RLL}}(t)\):
1. is consistent with zero within error bars;
2. has a preferred sign;
3. has a measurable amplitude;
4. depends on velocity, plasma, or superposition terms;
5. scales with redshift or environmental density.

## Refutation criterion
If RLL predicts a non-zero residual and observations constrain
\[
|\Delta\alpha_{\mathrm{RLL}}| < \sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}
\]
then that RLL parameter region is ruled out.

## Datasets / instruments
- Hubble astrometric microlensing
- Gaia proper-motion catalogues
- JWST follow-up of runaway black-hole candidates
- Roman Space Telescope future microlensing surveys
- LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA compact-object population priors

## Coerência × Amor × Prova (operational note)
- **Coerência:** separate static gravitational lensing from motion/environment terms.
- **Amor:** state uncertainty honestly and avoid overclaiming beyond data quality.
- **Prova:** publish falsifiable residual tests with uncertainty budgets and fit assumptions.
